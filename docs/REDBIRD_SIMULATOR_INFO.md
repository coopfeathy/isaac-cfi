# Redbird Flight Simulator Overview

## What is a Redbird Flight Simulator?

The Redbird FMX is an advanced, FAA-approved flight simulator designed for realistic pilot training. It provides:

- **Motion-Based Platform**: Six-axis motion system that replicates the actual feel of flying
- **High-Fidelity Graphics**: Photorealistic scenery and accurate aircraft behavior
- **Full Glass Cockpit**: Modern avionics suite identical to real aircraft
- **Weather Simulation**: Real-world weather patterns and effects
- **Day & Night Operations**: Full 24-hour capability including instruments flying
- **Runway Selection**: Over 40,000 airports available worldwide

## FAA Qualification and Approval

- Device family approved by FAA as an Advanced Aviation Training Device (AATD): Redbird LD, SD, FMX, MCX
- Approval basis: 14 CFR 61.4(c) and AC 61-136
- FAA LOA reference date: October 21, 2021
- Stated LOA expiration date: October 31, 2026

## Important FAA Limitations

- Cross-country, night, solo, takeoffs/landings, and recent practical test prep requirements must be completed in aircraft
- No portion of a practical test or type-specific training credit can be conducted in an AATD
- The flight portion of a flight review under 14 CFR 61.56(a) cannot be completed in an AATD
- The LOA and approved Qualification and Approval Guide (QAG) must be readily available near the device
- Only an appropriately qualified FAA-certificated instructor may make instructional endorsements/logbook entries when used for instruction
