# KPNE vs Wings Field vs Doylestown: Which Philly Airport Is Best for Student Pilots?

*by Isaac Prestwich, CFII — owner of Merlin Flight Training at KPNE*

If you live in the Philadelphia metro and you're shopping for a flight school, you have realistically three choices for where to learn: **Northeast Philadelphia Airport (KPNE)**, **Wings Field (KLOM)**, and **Doylestown Airport (KDYL)**. There are smaller fields too (Brandywine, Quakertown), but these three are where most student pilots actually train.

I run a flight school at KPNE, so my bias is obvious. But I've flown out of all three. Here's the honest comparison.

## The three airports at a glance

| | KPNE | KLOM (Wings Field) | KDYL (Doylestown) |
|---|---|---|---|
| Location | Northeast Philadelphia | Blue Bell, Montgomery County | Doylestown, Bucks County |
| Drive from Center City | ~25 min | ~35 min | ~45 min |
| Drive from Cherry Hill | ~30 min | ~45 min | ~50 min |
| Drive from King of Prussia | ~30 min | ~10 min | ~30 min |
| Runway length | 7,000 ft + 5,000 ft | 3,700 ft | 3,500 ft |
| Control tower | Yes (Class D) | Yes (Class D) | No (uncontrolled, Class G) |
| Field elevation | 120 ft | 302 ft | 393 ft |
| Approaches | ILS, RNAV, multiple | RNAV | RNAV |
| Type of operation | Mixed GA + corporate | Mostly GA training | Mostly GA training |

## Where each one wins

### KPNE — Northeast Philadelphia Airport

**Wins for:** Center City Philadelphia residents, Northeast Philly residents, South Jersey commuters, anyone who wants to train at a towered field with long runways and instrument approaches without paying for it.

**Why:** KPNE has a 7,000-foot main runway — far longer than you'll ever need in a Cheetah or 172. That's great for two reasons: (1) you have huge margins for landing errors as a student, and (2) you'll get exposure to operations with corporate jets and Pilatuses, which is genuinely good training. The full ILS approach also means once you start instrument training, you can practice real approaches at your home field instead of flying somewhere else.

**Watch out for:** Class D tower means radio work from day one. Some students like this (forces them to get good at comms early); others find it intimidating. If you're radio-shy, you'll get over it faster here than at an untowered field.

### KLOM — Wings Field

**Wins for:** Montgomery County residents in King of Prussia / Conshohocken / Plymouth Meeting, families with kids who like the small-field vibe.

**Why:** Wings is a beloved local field with a strong community. Shorter runway (3,700 ft) means you'll be flying tight patterns and managing energy carefully — both good skills. Class D tower like KPNE, but lower traffic.

**Watch out for:** Wings is expensive. Both aircraft rental and instruction trend higher than KPNE-area rates because it's an upscale field. The shorter runway also limits which aircraft can comfortably train there. And you're farther from anything east of the Schuylkill — South Jersey students drive past Wings on their way home.

### KDYL — Doylestown Airport

**Wins for:** Bucks County residents, anyone who wants a quieter, lower-traffic environment to learn in.

**Why:** Doylestown is untowered (Class G), which some instructors think is the *best* place to learn for the first 10–20 hours. No radio pressure. You just announce your position on CTAF and fly. Field has its own community vibe.

**Watch out for:** Untowered also means you have to start over with radio work eventually when you fly into a controlled field for solo cross-countries or your checkride. Some students find this jarring. KDYL also has the shortest runway of the three (3,500 ft) and the highest field elevation (393 ft), which means slightly thinner air on hot summer days — performance-wise no big deal for a primary trainer, but it's a real factor on density-altitude days.

## What actually matters when picking

Three things most students don't think about until it's too late:

### 1. Drive time

If you have to drive 50 minutes each way to your flight school, **you will quit**. I've seen it dozens of times. Cancel the lesson because of weather? You just wasted two hours of your day driving for nothing. Schedule a sunset flight? You'll get home at 10 PM and resent it.

Pick the closest viable airport. Lessons are 1.5–2 hours of actual airport time; if you're adding 100 minutes of driving on top, you'll burn out before you finish.

### 2. Instructor availability

A great instructor at a less-convenient field beats a mediocre instructor down the street. But "great" doesn't mean "the most-credentialed person on the website" — it means the person who will actually fly with you twice a week consistently for the next 6 months.

Ask any flight school: *"If I sign up tomorrow, which CFI will I be paired with, and how many other students do they currently have?"* If the school can't answer that in 30 seconds, you'll spend half your training getting bounced between instructors, which adds 20% to your total cost.

### 3. Plane availability

A school with 1 airplane and 8 students is going to disappoint you. A school with 4 airplanes and 12 students is going to be great. Always ask: *"On a typical Saturday, how many aircraft are out and how many students are on the schedule?"*

Quick honest disclosure on this one: Merlin runs a single airplane (the Grumman Cheetah AA-5A). We're small on purpose — Isaac is the only CFI, and we cap the student roster so every student gets time on the plane every week. If you want a school with 4 airplanes and a roster of 30+, we're not it. If you want a 1-on-1 model where the owner is also your instructor, we are.

Merlin Flight Training operates a **Grumman Cheetah AA-5A** out of KPNE. Most other Philly-area schools fly Cessna 172s. The Cheetah is sportier, faster, and (in my opinion) teaches you to fly the airplane rather than fly past your mistakes. It's well-maintained and IFR-equipped.

## How to decide

If you live in:

- **Center City, Northeast Philly, Bensalem, South Jersey, Delaware:** KPNE is closest. Visit Merlin or another KPNE school first.
- **King of Prussia, Conshohocken, Plymouth Meeting:** Wings (KLOM) is closest. Visit there first.
- **Doylestown, Newtown, central Bucks County:** Doylestown (KDYL) is closest. Visit there first.

But before you commit, **do a discovery flight at one of them**. One hour in the air tells you more than ten hours of YouTube research. If you book one at Merlin, you'll be at the controls within 15 minutes of arrival, fly over Center City Philadelphia and the Delaware River, and be home in 2 hours total.
